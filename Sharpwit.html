Sharpwit

I remember I
It was 1964 when Sharpwit first appeared; We were having English class when he turned up; everything went black and dark as the alarms went off. We ran. We all ran; we all ran the wrong way: Into the mouth of the lion. Suddenly I heard a cry from the front; the voice was tortured and crackled: “RUN-NNNNNNNNNNNN” People started running backwards like the waves; pushing up the beach and escaping back. I finally understood what was happening, but it was just too late. The running waves pinched me down to the cold tiles of the floor. When the waves finally left, it was only me and my fallen friends on the floor, and one another; a tall, sleek and dark person. I couldn’t see his face clearly until he pulled his hood off; he was handsome, yet creepy, like Tom Marvolo Riddle in the famous Harry Potter Series from the FUTURE. I knew who he was: Sharpwit the Lord of the dark wisdom.
He drew his swords; not one, but two. He slashed it through the boy closest to him. The boy; dazed; fell to the floor, dead. But there was no cut revealed on him. I understood; Sharpwit killed his soul. I started to run. Several seconds later, sharpwit's airborne sword already ran through most of my friend’s soul.
“He’s coming for me” I thought with fear. I tripped, fell to the floor flat. And was pinched to the floor. For the first time in my life, I started reviewing the messy work of art. The sun from the view of the beach finally descended down the horizon of the west; the sadness of a breakup leaves like a shadow walking away from the light.
“It’s finished, finally” I tried to relax, but there is no gaps between the door and the wall. The blade went down my arm. I screamed with my highest pitch. No one came. Then it was gone. Gone. Gone.
I remember II
I woke up in the busy hospital. People swarmed around me. I wiggled my toe to see if it was a dream; It was NOT. Then cheer flew up from my toe to my bulging brain: I’m alive! Then I remembered the darkness. My arm, my precious arm! I looked right to my hurt arm. I can see it. But I can’t feel it there. I tried to move it. It was like trying to flap your ears. My heart swiveled with coldness. The poor arm. It's there, and it isn't there.
I tried to cheer myself up, but I can’t.
“How could I work, how could I eat? 'Eating is the only thing that holds people's hopeful heart up!' said Chairman Mao.”
Then I looked around at the environment around me. The high ceilings and the fogged windows. I stretched my neck to inspect the ground, covered with pearl white tiles, younger than the ones in the accident. Between the tiles was a small aisle of dirty mud. I looked around me: worried nurses pushing carts filled with medicines and sad people crouching over beds, weeping. I sort of felt sorry for them. Chances are, the people they are weeping for have their souls dead. I am lucky. Only my right arm is dead; at least I’m alive, the only survivor attacked by Sharpwit.
School
School went perfectly normal except some students are chatting about the event that happened.
“They weren’t scared about Sharpwit, are they?” I said to my friend, Joe Taylor.
“They are, but they wouldn’t show it, Howard.” Joe said.
I hesitated before saying this, “Don’t tell anyone about this, okay?” I pointed at my arm, which I didn’t feel at all. Joe simply nodded. We went for the "path as long as a dragon" to the lockers. The students dimmed out as we passed the place where Sharpwit attacked. I saw silvery blood on the cold floor. I paused. Joe kept walking.
“JOE” I shouted, “There is BLOOD”
“Blood? I don’t see anything!” Joe said innocently.
“I promise, there is blood!” I said.
“I don’t see anything. You should probably go to the hospital.”
I saw green, transparent bodies on the ground. Some of them are only half a body, though. They were my killed friends! The I found an arm. I knew who’s arm it was, it's mine.
"MY-yyyyyy Preshousssss" I croaked slowly like playing jazz ballad.
I grabbed it; Half expecting I could touch it. To my amazement, I lifted it up and stuffed it in my bag.
“Joe!” I shouted. I ran to our lockers. But he wasn’t there.
“He must have gone to the bus.” I thought to myself and went left into the library.
Esmeralda Carmentor
 I walked into the nearly-empty library. I saw the librarian on the far side packing up her handbag. She carried some heavy books and left. Then I looked to my left. There was a young girl, sitting there. She was reading a thick book called《War&Peace》. She sat there, calmly. Her tiny bag was sat just right beside her. Uncontrollably, I walked towards her. She looked up; her eyes were friendly; blue and pure like a galaxy spread wide; shining. A strange feeling surged up me.
“Hi” I said shyly.
“Hi” She said equally shyly.
“I’m called Howard. Howard Smith.” I said.
“I’m called Esmeralda Carmentor.” She said back.
“Aren’t you scared?” I asked, pushing the limit a bit more.
“About what?”
“About Sharpwit!” I said, amazed.
“I am. But there is one thing that you can do.”
“What if he comes again?” I said.
“Your arm!” She exclaimed “it wasn’t moving.” She suddenly jumped off the topic.
“I’m okay.” I said sarcastically.
She grinned. I took a book as thick as her book.
“Dream of the red chamber,” She said, “It’s a good book. I like it.”
We sat on the same couch together until 6pm.
“I have to go.” I said.
“Me too.”
We packed our bags and went the different way.
The road of dawn
I was walking home with my heavy bag. I know what the feeling is. I like her. I never got a friend since I was four. She’s so calm. The road under me seems to announce the prophecy, “You two belong together.” I don’t think it was real but I walked on. The sun was setting as sad birds flew past.
“She’ll never like me.” I said, “I even lost an arm.”
I walked past the stream under the bridge. It was nearly dried out. The autumn leaves fell off the forlorn trees; the small pieces of paint fell of the melancholy building.
“I said it was the end. It wasn’t”

School II
I walked to the first day of our lesson. I was bright and happy because of meeting with Esmeralda. I didn’t know what was going to happen next; it was something terrible. We did everything normal. I sat in my normal seat. SecondIy, I was staring at Esmeralda. She walked to a seat right behind me. Still carrying the colossal book. Things were just normal as it should be until the world is greyish and dark. We couldn’t see any colours except for black and white. The alarms went off. Screams echoed through the endless corridors congested with frightened students. Sharpwit attacked the school again. I span and ran for my dear life. Sprinted, I did. I didn't care about anything before I remembered about Esmeralda.

"Esme-" I shouted before I controlled myself. I tried to find out where Esmeralda was. I looked to the left, and behind. Then I turned and gave my last try. To the left. I saw the back of a tiny figure and a dark tower. Pulling two sticks out of his long, sloppy pocket. 
"ESMERALDA!" I shouted, finnaly out of control and I sprinted to the frightened girl. I grabbed a mop from my left and dashed like a sauring Pagani Huayra that has a jammed engine. You're right. I fell of my balance several meters before Lord Sharpwit. 
"It's you." Said Sharpwit coldly, "The only survivor I attacked. Today, I shall kill you and that useless girl. !" I grabbed the mop with my saint-like left hand. WHACK! The weak mop attacked the the solid swords. The result was not surprising, the mop was chopped clear in half. I expected a miracle to help me, but nothing did at this moment. I picked the second part of the mop up and fought fiercely, but it just broke down more. Soon the exausted mop was overpowered by the sword. His sword slashed several centimeters besides me and I fell to the floor. I crawled back like a jerk. The blade went down beside me again.

I didn't believe it when I saw it," ESMERALDA!!!!!!!!!!!" I ran forward. She said, "Howard. Remember...." She fell silent before she could finish. Tears fell out my eye sockets
